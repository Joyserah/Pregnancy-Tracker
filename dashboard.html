Pregnancy Care for Pulmonary Hypertension

About Your Journey

Pulmonary hypertension means the blood vessels in your lungs are a little tighter than usual, so your heart has to work harder to move blood through them. During pregnancy, your body naturally works extra hard, and this condition can make those changes feel more noticeable.

This app gently guides you through understanding your condition, tracking how you feel, and staying connected with your care team. It's here to help you breathe easier, feel supported, and move through pregnancy with confidence and calm.

📊 Daily Health Log

Why Track These Numbers?
Because your heart and lungs work a little harder during pregnancy, keeping an eye on a few simple numbers can help your care team support you and your baby. Many women with pulmonary hypertension are asked to check things like:

Blood pressure — often checked regularly at home so your team can spot changes early.
Blood sugar (glucose) — checked as recommended, especially if there are concerns about gestational diabetes.
Weight — checked often to watch for fluid changes, which can be important in pulmonary hypertension.

Your care team will tell you exactly how often to check each one, and this app helps you track everything in one calm, easy place. Think of it as a gentle way to stay connected to your health between appointments.
